Neer Dosa

Description

Neer Dosa ("water dosa" in Tulu) is a soft, thin, lacy crepe from Coastal Karnataka. Made from fermented rice batter.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice (Sona Masuri or similar)
  • 1/2 cup grated fresh coconut (optional but recommended)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed (about 2–3 cups for thin batter)
  • Oil or ghee for greasing the pan

Steps

  1. Soak the rice in water for 4–6 hours (or overnight).
  2. Drain and grind the rice with coconut (if using) and salt to a very smooth paste, adding water gradually.
  3. Add more water to make a very thin, runny batter (like milk consistency). Let it rest 30 minutes.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan on medium heat. Lightly grease with oil.
  5. Pour a ladle of batter in the center and swirl gently to spread thinly (no need to flip).
  6. Cover and cook 1–2 minutes until edges lift and top looks cooked. Fold and serve hot with coconut chutney or chicken curry.
